Research Overview

My research program is dedicated to enhancing livestock productivity and public health safety through the lens of Veterinary Epidemiology. By integrating field-level disease investigation with advanced data analytics, my work aims to identify the risk factors that govern animal health in the unique ecological and socio-economic landscape of Bangladesh.

I utilize a "One Health" approach, recognizing the link between animal health, human nutrition, and the environment. Using tools like STATA, ArcGIS, and Epi Info, I provide scientific frameworks to reduce disease burden and minimize antimicrobial dependency.

Selected Research Projects

Epidemiological Investigations & Disease Surveillance

  • Principal Investigator: Nationwide longitudinal study on Buffalo Diseases (2021–2026) focusing on disease prevalence and socio-economic impact.
  • Bovine Ephemeral Fever (BEF) Surveillance: Seroprevalence studies and molecular characterization for better diagnostic strategies.
  • Vaccine Efficacy Trials: Evaluating field-level effectiveness of livestock vaccines for national recommendation programs.

Dairy Science & Food Safety

  • UKRI GCRF One Health Poultry Hub: Investigating food safety and zoonotic transmission in the livestock value chain.
  • Udder Health Management: Implementing "Decision Tree" protocols to manage subclinical mastitis and improve farm profitability.
  • Heavy Metal Contamination: Researching lead (Pb) poisoning risks in the livestock food chain.

Sustainable Livestock Intensification

  • Buffalo Value Chain Analysis: Climate change mitigation through sustainable water buffalo dairy chains.
  • Antibiotic Stewardship: Designing strategies for effective production with reduced antimicrobial use (SLU Sweden Standards).
