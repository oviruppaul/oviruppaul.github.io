Welcome

I am Ovirup Bhushan Paul, a Veterinarian and Epidemiologist dedicated to strengthening the livestock sector in Bangladesh through a unique blend of scientific rigor and field-level expertise. Currently serving as a Scientific Officer at the Bangladesh Livestock Research Institute (BLRI), my career is driven by a singular mission: to translate complex epidemiological data into sustainable, high-yield outcomes for the dairy industry.

My journey began at Chattogram Veterinary and Animal Sciences University, where I developed a deep-seated interest in population health and infectious disease dynamics. This path led me to specialize in MSc Epidemiology and pursue advanced international training in Sweden, focusing on bacteriological diagnostics and the global challenge of antimicrobial resistance. Today, I apply these insights to the specific challenges of the Bangladeshi landscape—from investigating buffalo disease trends to optimizing the health of commercial dairy herds.

What sets my work apart is the balance between the laboratory and the farm. While my academic efforts contribute to global knowledge on udder health and milk quality, my field-work is deeply rooted in the commercial reality of Bangladesh’s dairy farmers. I specialize in providing evidence-based Services that prioritize animal welfare and farm profitability, ensuring that modern veterinary science is accessible and actionable for local stakeholders.

As you explore this site, you will find my Research into buffalo production, a catalog of my Publications in high-impact journals, and details on my professional Services for commercial operations. Whether you are a fellow researcher, a policy-maker, or a farm owner, I look forward to connecting and collaborating to build a more resilient and antibiotic-conscious future for our livestock sector.
