Welcome

I am Ovirup Bhushan Paul, a Veterinarian and Epidemiologist dedicated to strengthening the livestock sector in Bangladesh through a unique blend of scientific rigor and field-level expertise. Currently serving as a Scientific Officer at the Bangladesh Livestock Research Institute (BLRI), I am driven by a singular mission: to translate complex epidemiological data into sustainable, high-yield outcomes for the dairy industry.

My journey in veterinary science led me to specialize in MSc Epidemiology with advanced international training in Sweden. Today, I apply these insights to the specific challenges of the Bangladeshi landscape—from investigating buffalo disease trends to optimizing the health of commercial dairy herds.

What sets my work apart is the balance between the laboratory and the farm. While my academic efforts contribute to global knowledge on udder health, my fieldwork is deeply rooted in the commercial reality of Bangladesh’s dairy farmers. I provide evidence-based Services that prioritize animal welfare and farm profitability.

Explore my Research into buffalo production, my Publications in high-impact journals, and my professional Services for commercial operations. I look forward to collaborating toward a more resilient and antibiotic-conscious future for our livestock sector.
